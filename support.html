Support East Petersburg Historical Society


Join EPHS to benefit from:

  • Belonging to something meaningful – a group that values place, memory, and shared identity
  • Lifelong learning – Discover fascinating stories about your town, neighborhoods, and the people.
  • Intellectual stimulation – Talks, archives, and research keep curious minds engaged.
  • Social connection – Meet neighbors who care about history, culture, and preservation.
  • Pride of place – Gain a deeper appreciation for where you live and its unique character.

Exclusive Access & Experiences

  • Behind-the-scenes access – Archives, collections, historic homes, and sites not open to the public.
  • Special tours and trips – Walking tours, day trips, or travel tied to local history.
  • Early or discounted entry – Savings on programs, workshops, and ticketed events.

Impact & Legacy

  • Preserve local history – Help protect buildings, documents, photographs, and oral histories.
  • Leave a legacy – Contribute to something that will outlast you and benefit future generations.
  • Advocacy voice – Be part of efforts to protect historic places from being lost or forgotten.

Creative & Skill-Building Opportunities

  • Hands-on involvement – Research, cataloging, exhibits, writing, or restoration projects.
  • Share your talents – Photography, storytelling, teaching, design, genealogy, or event planning.
  • Learn new skills – Archival work, historical research, preservation techniques.

Family & Community Value

  • Family-friendly activities – Programs that make history engaging for kids and teens.
  • Intergenerational connection – Learn from longtime residents and elders.
  • Community storytelling – Help tell a fuller, more inclusive history of the area.

Practical Perks

  • Member discounts – Gift shop, publications, partner museums
  • Newsletter – High-quality local history content delivered regularly.
  • Recognition – Satisfaction (and sometimes public acknowledgment) for supporting a good cause.

Archival Materials

  • Photographs – Prints, negatives, slides, and digital images
          - optionally lend them for high-res scanning so originals are returned to you;
          - photos containing streets, businesses, buildings of the past are particulaly of interest!
  • Documents & Papers – Deeds, letters, diaries, ledgers, maps, and certificates
  • Manuscripts – Family histories, memoirs, and unpublished writings
  • Printed Materials – Programs, flyers, posters, and ephemera
  • Audio & Visual Media – Oral histories, recordings, films, and videotapes

Artifacts & Objects

  • Household Items – Kitchen tools, textiles, furnishings, and décor
  • Tools & Implements – Agricultural, craft, trade, or industrial tools
  • Personal Effects – Clothing, accessories, uniforms, and keepsakes
  • Community Objects – School, church, business, or civic organization items
  • Military & Service Items – Medals, equipment, and related memorabilia

Art & Visual Culture

  • Paintings & Drawings – Original works depicting local people or places
  • Prints & Illustrations – Engravings, lithographs, and reproduced artwork
  • Signage & Advertising – Store signs, product labels, and promotional materials

Books & Research Resources

  • Local History Publications – Books, pamphlets, and scholarly studies
  • Genealogical Records – Family trees, research notes, and compiled histories
  • Reference Materials – Atlases, directories, and specialized indexes

Digital & Contemporary Records

  • Digital Files – Scanned documents, photographs, and born-digital records
  • Web & Social Media Archives – Content documenting community life
  • Recent Materials – Items reflecting modern history and ongoing change

Monetary Gifts

  • Donations – Support preservation, conservation, and programming
  • Endowments & Bequests – Long-term support for the Society’s mission
  • Professional Services – Conservation, digitization, research, or design assistance
  • Supplies & Equipment – Archival storage materials, display cases, or technology

  • Learn specialized practices – Gain experience in archival handling, documentation, and interpretation.
  • Creative contribution – Assist with exhibits, writing, photography, or digital projects.

Community Impact

  • Preservation in action – Help safeguard historical materials for future generations.
  • Public-facing work – Support programs, tours, and educational outreach.
  • Support local storytelling – Help ensure community history is accurately recorded and shared.
